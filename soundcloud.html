The Research

Before getting started on this project, I wanted to get feedback from as many people as possible so that I could account for different user cases, from an artist using the service to upload new tracks to fans using the service to discover all sorts of remixes. I personally use the service to discover acoustic covers and indie music, but I wanted to enlarge my view of the service through this investigation. In order to be more inclusive with my design, I began researching what people loved about SoundCloud, and how the current application for Android impacted their SoundCloud experience. Living in NYC gave me a vast and diverse research pool from which I was able to gather qualitative data as to what current users actually think about the application. As my research developed, two problems became increasingly apparent regarding the current search module and the application’s navigation. With these issues in mind I formulated two important needs that applied to the user base’s needs. Two user needs. Two case scenarios.

*How can user discover great new content in a fun, dynamic, and meaningful way?

*And, how can a user easily access his/her profile application settings?

CRM

On the CRM team I worked with very talented communication designers on email designs and explorations. I worked on the design of "new" and "different" layouts for the announcement of new products for drivers and riders, the reason behind these explorations was to discover a more exiting look for the company's emails in order to increase their open rates and click through ratios.
